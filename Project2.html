Overview

In this page you will be shown how I used HTML to create a hangman game, with a functioning visual hangman, with 10 chances to guess a random 5 letter word, if the player guesses a letter wrong the letter will go into the already used letters group, and the player will lose 1 chance. If the player guesses correctly the letter that the player guesses will go into the already used group and the letter will go into the spot where it's supposed to be in blank spaces, that represents the 5 letter word.

The player loses if they lose all 10 of their chances and make the full hangman, and the player wins if they guess all the letters in the 5 letter word, without losing all 10 of their chances.

End user

My end users are children aged from 8-12 years old, who speak english, and who are looking for a way to waste time, learn how to spell, and have some fun doing it. I think that children will like my hangman game, because it is a fun learning experience, for children interested in learning how to spell. I have taken a more child friendly approach to making my game, because I made a visual hangman out of letters, so that the children will be entertained for longer. The outcome of the hangman game is not to make it too complex so that children can understand how to play and have fun. My hangman game could be used to entertain children if the parent wants a break and just wants to relax.
