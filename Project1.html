Overview

In this page you will be shown how I used blender to create a 3d low poly character and a running animation for it, in year 11 Digitech. This includes a design/model on the 3d low poly character, and also includes a running animation that I made for it.

End user

My end users are children and teenagers aged around 8-12 years old, who speak english, and who are looking for a 3d low poly medieval knight. I think that children will like my 3d low poly knight because they like stuff like firefighters, police men, and knights because I liked them when i was a child. I have taken a less detailed approach to cr eating it. The outcome of the armor is more towards the simple side, because it's more blocky and sharp rather than smooth. The low poly knight could be used as a character for anyone who is making a medieva l game. My low poly character is a rather tall, and slim person, covered from head to toe in silver armor.

Requirements

Head/Helmet

  • The head is completely covered by a silver knight's helmet.
  • The helmet is a barbute helmet with a T-shaped opening for the eyes.
  • The T-shaped opening is filled in with black.
  • Rigging that connect the head to the body

Torso

  • Rigging that is connecting the torso to the arms, legs, and head.
  • The torso is completely covered by silver armor from front to back.
  • The armor has a dominant chest piece that sticks out a bit from the lower parts of the torso armor.

Arms/SHolders

  • There are 3 joints, the shoulder, elbow, and wrist, rigging will connect them all together.
  • The shoulders have silver shoulder pads, the left one larger than the other.
  • There are silver shoulder pads.
  • There is silver armor covering the rest of the arms and hands.

Legs

  • There are 3 joints, the hips, knee, and the ankle, rigging will connect them all together.
  • There is a brown belt that is a sort of divider between the legs and the torso.
  • There are silver knee pads.
  • There are silver shoes
  • There is silver armor covering the rest of the legs.

Rigging

  • The rigging is like the human nervous system, and muscles.
  • It connects all the joints together, the head to the torso, the torso to the arms, the elbows, the wrists, the torso to the legs, the knees, and the ankles.
